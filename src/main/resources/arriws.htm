Arrow (symbol)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Categories:

This article is about the symbol. For other uses, see Arrow (disambiguation).

This article contains special characters. Without proper rendering support, you may see question marks, boxes, or other symbols.

An arrow is a graphical symbol such as or , used to point or indicate direction, being in its simplest form a line segment with a triangle affixed to one end, and in more complex forms a representation of an actual arrow (e.g. U+27B5). The direction indicated by an arrow is the one along the length of the line towards the end capped by a triangle. Arrows are also used, in some cultures, as a sign of danger, depending on the direction the arrow is pointing.

[edit] North arrow

Main article: Compass rose

North arrow is used in floor plans and maps to indicate the direction to the north.
